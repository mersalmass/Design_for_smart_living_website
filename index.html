Design for Smart Living

Beyond Convenience

About The Course

In this studio-based course, students learn core interaction design principles for smart homes, cities, and interconnected lifestyles while critically examining the social, ethical, and human dimensions of technology. They explore diverse frameworks, including Gandhian perspectives on autonomy and resilience, to question whose definitions of smart are prioritized and what values are embedded in design. Through hands-on projects and speculative future-casting, students create prototypes and concepts for smart living that are not only technologically innovative but also socially responsible, ethically grounded, and aimed at enhancing human flourishing.
